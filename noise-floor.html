NOISE
FLOOR

Undermining the status quo through sonic experimentation and frequency resistance.

Silence is a myth. In the cracks of the modern machine, a new frequency is emerging. The Noise Floor is not just a technical threshold—it is a site of resistance, a layer of sonic grit that refuses to be filtered by the algorithms of comfort.

The Static Revolution

We have been taught to seek "clean" signals. Every software update promising less noise, more clarity, and a smoother experience. But in this sanitized digital landscape, we lose the texture of existence. The Noise Floor project explores the aesthetic potential of the unwanted, the discarded, and the distorted.

By amplifying the low-level hum of electrical grids and the packet loss of wireless networks, we transform the invisible infrastructure of our city into a visceral musical experience. This is not about melody; it is about the physical impact of sound waves that have no destination.

Our instruments are the very devices meant to keep the signal pure. Through circuit bending and deliberate feedback loops, we force the technology to reveal its true, chaotic nature. The result is a sonic landscape that mirrors the fragmentation of the digital self.

Sonic Sabotage

To listen to the Noise Floor is to engage in a form of sonic sabotage. It is a refusal to accept the pre-packaged, high-fidelity world we are sold. Instead, we find beauty in the glitch, the crackle, and the roar of a system pushing past its limits.

In the reverse-dual models of auditory processing, the brain often attempts to filter out background noise to focus on a primary source. Our goal is to flip this relationship, making the background the foreground, and the "signal" nothing more than a distraction.

As we push deeper into these frequencies, the boundaries between the physical and the digital begin to blur. The vibration of a speaker becomes the vibration of the architecture, turning the entire room into a resonant chamber of deliberate failure.
